ONAtrail · MAGIC-Trail Research Project · Last updated 2025

1. Who We Are

ONAtrail is a web platform developed as part of the MAGIC-Trail research project, led by Dr. Nicolas Bouscaren at the Reunion University Hospital (CHU de La Réunion) and funded through the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF/FEDER).

The platform provides trail runners with tools for race preparation, performance analysis, pace optimization, training monitoring, and the opportunity to participate in research projects related to trail running and endurance sports.

3. Data We Collect

Depending on the services you use, we may collect:

  • Account information: first name, last name, email address, age, and sex.
  • Connection identifiers for Garmin and Strava synchronization (we never collect your Garmin or Strava usernames or passwords).
  • Sports activity data: distance, duration, elevation gain, GPS tracks, pace, heart rate, training load, and other available performance indicators.
  • Health-related data: heart rate, heart rate variability, recovery and sleep metrics when available through connected services, and injury history provided through questionnaires.
  • Research questionnaire responses: epidemiological questionnaires, satisfaction surveys, etc.
  • Technical connection data: IP address, connection logs, and session identifiers.
Age requirement: The minimum age for registration is 18 years old. Access for minors may be granted upon request and is subject to specific consent requirements, including parental consent where applicable, in accordance with Article 8 of the GDPR and the digital age of consent established under French law.

4. Purposes and Legal Bases for Processing

In accordance with Article 13(1)(c) of the GDPR, each processing activity relies on a distinct legal basis:

  • Account creation and management, platform operation, display and analysis of your activities → performance of a contract (Article 6(1)(b) GDPR).
  • Personalized training, pacing, and performance recommendations → performance of a contract (Article 6(1)(b) GDPR), and, for AI-based functionalities that rely on health data, explicit consent (Article 9(2)(a) GDPR).
  • Participation in scientific research involving health data → explicit and specific consent for research purposes (Article 9(2)(a) GDPR), separate from any cookie-related consent and separate from contractual consent.
  • Security, fraud prevention, and technical maintenance → legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR).
  • Compliance with legal and regulatory obligations → legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c) GDPR).
The MAGIC-Trail / ONAtrail project has received a favorable opinion from the appropriate Ethics Committee (Comité de Protection des Personnes – CPP) and has been declared in accordance with the requirements of the French Data Protection Authority (CNIL).

5. Use of Artificial Intelligence

ONAtrail may use activity data and certain health-related data to develop, train, improve, or operate analytical, predictive, and recommendation models — for example: pacing recommendations, injury risk estimation, and training load analysis.

Whenever such processing involves health data, your prior and explicit consent is systematically required. This requirement is not conditional but arises directly from Article 9(2) of the GDPR whenever special categories of personal data are processed.

You may withdraw your consent at any time through your account settings or by contacting us, without affecting access to the platform's essential functionalities. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before such withdrawal.

6. Recipients of Personal Data

Depending on the purposes described above, your data may be shared with:

  • Researchers and scientific staff involved in the MAGIC-Trail project, strictly within the scope of authorized research activities.
  • Institutional partners and project funders (including within the framework of the ERDF/FEDER program), in aggregated and anonymized form unless otherwise provided for in the research protocol.
  • Technical service providers (hosting, maintenance, and infrastructure services), acting as data processors within the meaning of Article 28 GDPR and bound by appropriate data processing agreements.
  • Garmin and Strava, solely to the extent necessary to synchronize the data that you explicitly authorize us to access. These companies operate under their own terms and privacy policies.
We do not sell your personal data. Aggregated and anonymized data may be used for research, statistical analysis, and platform improvement purposes.

7. Data Hosting

Personal health data are hosted by Orange Business, a provider certified as a Health Data Hosting Provider (Hébergeur de Données de Santé – HDS) in accordance with Article L.1111-8 of the French Public Health Code.

8. Data Retention

Your account data are retained for as long as your account remains active and are deleted or anonymized within one week following account closure.

Data collected as part of scientific research activities are retained for the duration of the MAGIC-Trail project and subsequently archived in accordance with applicable retention requirements governing health research.

You may request the deletion of your account and associated personal data at any time, subject to any legal retention obligations or requirements necessary to preserve the integrity of an ongoing research protocol.

10. Data Security

Technical and organizational measures are implemented to protect your data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction:

  • Encryption of data both in transit and at rest
  • Strict access control mechanisms
  • Audit logging and traceability of access to health data
  • Secure user authentication procedures
  • Regular backup procedures
